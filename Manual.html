⬡ OVERVIEW

Stellar Drift is a mobile VR survival game built entirely in A-Frame. You pilot a drifting spacecraft through two escalating levels of asteroid fields and a planetary boss encounter — using only your gaze and head movement, no hands required.

Collect floating gem crystals to build your score, dodge incoming asteroids, and survive long enough to face the Level 2 boss. Defeat it by locking your gaze onto its glowing eye to deal damage. All audio, textures, and visuals are generated programmatically — no external asset files required.

VR Survival / Score Attack

Mobile Browser + Google Cardboard / Any WebXR HMD

⬡ INSTALLATION & SETUP

Option A — Play Directly (No Install)

Open your mobile browser (Chrome recommended) and navigate to the GitHub Pages URL.

Tap the screen on the splash screen to proceed to the Setup menu.

Configure your preferences (difficulty, audio, stereoscopic mode).

Tap LAUNCH GAME. If stereoscopic is enabled, insert your phone into a Google Cardboard headset.

Use the VR icon (⊕) in the bottom-right corner of A-Frame to toggle stereo mode manually anytime.

Option B — Run Locally

Clone or download the repository:

git clone https://github.com/yourusername/stellar-drift.git
cd stellar-drift

Serve with any static server. Python example:

# Python 3
python3 -m http.server 8080

# Node.js (npx)
npx serve .

Open http://localhost:8080 in your browser.

For mobile testing on the same Wi-Fi network, use your computer's local IP (e.g. http://192.168.x.x:8080) on your phone.

⬡ CONTROLS

Stellar Drift uses gaze-based and head-tilt controls exclusively — no hands, no controllers, no keyboard (except on desktop for testing).

Control Action How It Works
👁 Gaze Target & collect gems Look at a gem for 1.2 s – the cursor fills and triggers collection
👁 Gaze (Boss) Damage boss eye Lock gaze on the red eye sphere to deal a hit
↕ Head Tilt (pitch) Move forward/drift Tilt head >15° down/up to drift in that direction
↔ Head Turn (yaw) Steer left/right Combined with tilt for directional drift
📱 Phone Tap (Cardboard) Confirm / interact Tap the Cardboard button to trigger gaze events instantly

💡 TIP: Cursor Ring

A teal ring appears in the center of your view. When you look at an interactive object, the ring shrinks — hold your gaze still until it fully collapses to activate.

⬡ LEVELS

LEVEL 1

Asteroid Field – Survive & Collect

Dodge incoming rocky asteroids spawned from all directions. Collect blue and golden gem crystals orbiting around you to score points. Blue gems: +10 pts  |  Gold gems: +25 pts  |  Gaze bonus: ×2

Survive for 60 seconds (Normal) to advance to Level 2.

LEVEL 2

Boss Encounter – The Crimson Giant

A massive planet-like boss orbits you while firing asteroid volleys at double speed. The boss has a glowing red eye — stare at it to deal damage. Deal 5 gaze hits to destroy it and win the game.

Survive 90 seconds OR defeat the boss to win. Either way: +1000 bonus pts

Tip: The boss orbits, so patience and tracking are key. Collectibles still spawn.

⬡ TECHNICAL FEATURES

Procedural Textures

All textures are generated in-browser using the HTML5 Canvas API — no image files required. This includes: rocky asteroid textures with random craters and highlights, gem crystal textures with radial gradients and facets, a planetary boss texture with atmospheric banding, and a starfield texture.

Procedural Audio

All sound is synthesized using the Web Audio API. The background music uses oscillators and noise buffers to create an ambient space atmosphere. Sound effects cover: gem collection (ascending tones), asteroid collision (low distorted burst), level-up (ascending arpeggio), and game over (descending drone).

Collision Detection

Each game tick, all active asteroids and collectibles are checked against the player's world position using 3D Euclidean distance. Asteroids within size + 0.8 units trigger a life penalty; collectibles within 1.5 units are auto-collected.

Gaze Interaction

A-Frame's built-in cursor component with fuse: true; fuseTimeout: 1200 implements dwell-based gaze selection. The cursor ring animates as it fills, providing clear feedback. Interactive objects include gem crystals and the boss eye.

Head-Tilt Control

A custom A-Frame component (head-drift) reads the camera's pitch and yaw each frame. When pitch exceeds 15°, the player's rig entity drifts in the corresponding direction — allowing hands-free navigation.

Stereoscopic View

Tapping Launch Game with the stereoscopic toggle enabled calls scene.enterVR(), triggering A-Frame's built-in WebXR stereoscopic split-screen mode, compatible with Google Cardboard and any WebXR-capable HMD.
